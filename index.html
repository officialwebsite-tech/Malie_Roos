Get to Know Me

About Me

I'm a Political Science and Spanish student at BYU, passionate about international affairs, humanitarian service, and building meaningful connections across cultures. My experience spans legal support, research, writing, education, and community outreach — case files at an immigration law firm, lesson plans for ESL students, feature stories for BYU's College of Life Sciences — and along the way I've built strong skills in communication, organization, and problem-solving.

During my humanitarian internship in Asunción, Paraguay, and my service with The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, I worked with diverse communities, taught English and self-reliance classes, coordinated humanitarian initiatives, and strengthened my Spanish through daily immersion. That experience shaped how I show up in a room: I listen first, translate when it's needed — literally and otherwise — and look for the practical next step that moves a project or a person forward.

I'm eager to keep growing professionally while contributing to organizations focused on global engagement, community development, and creating lasting, positive impact.

The main way to understand the world is to experience it. Through language, relationships, culture, and service, the world becomes our campus — that's where we begin to understand one another.
